One of the biggest mistakes we hear from women over 40 is: "It's too late for me to start." It's simply not true. Pilates reformer is one of the
workouts that fits the body of a woman at this age _best_ — and there are clear physiological reasons why.

## What Happens to Your Body After 40

After 40, and especially around menopause, several processes accelerate:

- **Muscle mass loss (sarcopenia)**: The body loses up to 1-2% of muscle mass per year without training
- **Bone density decrease**: Without stimulation, bones weaken
- **Hormonal changes**: Declining oestrogen affects flexibility, strength, and mood
- **Pain and stiffness**: Joints and muscles respond less well to neglect

The good news: **every one of these processes can be slowed and improved with the right exercise**.

## Why Pilates Reformer Is the Answer

### Building Muscle Mass Safely

The reformer allows full strength training without joint loading. This is essential — because joints that already feel strain at 40 don't need more
stress.

### Keeping Bones Strong

Working with resistance directly stimulates bones to maintain and build density. This is active osteoporosis prevention.

### Flexibility Without Risk

Pilates lengthens muscles and improves joint mobility safely and in a controlled way — unlike extreme stretching that can cause injury.

### Improved Posture and Quality of Life

Good posture means less pain, more energy, and looking years younger.

### Reducing Menopause Symptoms

Studies show regular exercise reduces hot flashes, improves sleep quality, and elevates mood — all common menopause symptoms.
