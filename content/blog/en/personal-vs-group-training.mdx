One of the first questions women ask when they come to the studio: "Should I start with private sessions or join a group?" There's no single right
answer, it depends on who you are, where you are, and what your goals are.

## Private Sessions – What They Offer

### Full Attention

When you're one-on-one with the instructor, every second is dedicated to you. Every movement is checked, every error corrected, every small success
identified and reinforced. Progress in this format is faster.

### Complete Customisation

Do you have knee pain? A history of back injury? Pregnant? Postpartum? In a private session the programme is built precisely for you, you won't need
to skip exercises because of limitations.

### Your Own Pace

Not too fast, not too slow. The pace is tailored exactly to your level and how you feel that day.

### Ideal for:

- Complete beginners
- People with injuries or limitations
- Those who want to progress quickly
- Those who prefer privacy

## Group Classes – What They Offer

### Group Energy

Something happens in a room with other people, energy, inspiration, community. Many of our clients say the group is what motivates them to show up
even on days they don't feel like it.

### Lower Cost

A group class costs less than a private session, which allows training more frequently on the same budget.

### Positive Competition

Seeing others progress motivates us. Not from negative rivalry, but inspiration.

### Friendships and Social Support

Small groups become communities. Many of our clients have formed deep friendships through their classes.

### Ideal for:

- Those who've completed the beginner stage and have a foundation
- Those seeking community and energy
- Those who want to train frequently

## The Combination Approach – What Most of Our Clients Do

The best solution for most women: **start private, then combine**.

- First 1-2 months: private sessions to build correct technique
- After that: one private session + 1-2 group classes per week
- As needed: additional private sessions for periods of focused improvement
