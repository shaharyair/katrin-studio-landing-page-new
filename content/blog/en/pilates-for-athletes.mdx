Whether you run, play tennis, cycle, swim, or do any other sport, Pilates reformer training could be the most significant addition you make to your
training routine. Not as a replacement, but as a tool that improves everything you already do.

## Why Athletes Add Pilates to Their Routine

### Core Is Everything

In every sport, power originates from the core. A sprinter runs faster because her core stabilises her trunk. A tennis player hits harder because the
rotation comes from her core. A swimmer is more efficient because her core maintains body position.

Pilates reformer builds the **deep core**, the layer beneath the visible abdominal muscles, and that's simply not achievable with conventional
strength training.

### Injury Prevention

Most sports injuries occur because of:

- Imbalance between left and right muscles
- Some muscles too strong, others too weak
- Insufficient flexibility
- Inefficient movement patterns

Pilates identifies and corrects these imbalances before they become injuries.

### Faster Recovery

If you're already injured, Pilates reformer is the rehabilitation tool of choice for physiotherapists. The spring resistance allows safe work on
injured areas while the rest of the body keeps training.

### Improved Movement Efficiency

Pilates teaches the body to move more efficiently, using less energy to produce more power. Athletes report performance improvements in just the
first 4-6 weeks.

## Elite Athletes Who Swear by Pilates

Katie Ledecky, numerous professional tennis players, NFL and NBA stars, all report Pilates as a regular part of their elite preparation.
