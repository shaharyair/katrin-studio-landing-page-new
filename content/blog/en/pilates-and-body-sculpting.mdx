A question we hear often: "Does Pilates help you lose weight?" The honest answer is, it's complicated. Pilates alone won't burn as many calories as
running. But it can change your body in ways that nothing else can, and that may be far more important.

## What Pilates Does to Your Body

### Builds Lean Muscle Mass

Muscle burns calories even at rest. The more muscle mass you have, the higher your metabolism, even when you're sleeping. Pilates reformer builds
quality, long, lean muscle throughout the entire body.

### Changes the "Shape" of Your Body

Even without significant weight change, Pilates changes the shape of the body. Toned muscles create a completely different body line, compact,
elongated, and defined.

### Improves Posture Which Affects Appearance

Good posture makes you look slimmer and taller without losing a gram. This isn't an illusion, it's biomechanics.

### Reduces Bloating

Improved blood and lymphatic circulation reduces bloating, especially in the abdomen and limbs.

## What Pilates Doesn't Do

Pilates won't replace a balanced diet. You can't do a Pilates class, then eat a large meal, and expect results. But this is true of all physical
activity.

Pilates also won't burn calories at the rate of running, an average session burns 200-400 calories. But the change in muscle mass and metabolism
compensates over time.

## The Winning Combination

Our clients who see the best body sculpting results typically do:

- 2-3 Pilates reformer sessions per week
- Balanced nutrition (not a diet)
- Additional gentle cardio if they want extra calorie burn (walking, swimming)

## How Long Until You See Results?

- **Month 1**: Changed body sensation, clothes sit differently
- **Months 2-3**: Visible changes in body shape
- **Months 3-6**: Complete transformation of body composition, defined muscles, straight posture
