We live in a world of pressure. Work, family, managing a home, the news cycle — our bodies are often in a state of chronic stress. Pilates reformer
training is not just a physical workout — it's one of the most accessible tools for resetting the nervous system and improving mood.

## What Happens in the Body Under Stress

When we're stressed, the body releases cortisol — the stress hormone. At high, chronic levels, cortisol:

- Disrupts sleep
- Weakens the immune system
- Causes muscle pain and physical tension
- Leads to overeating and weight gain
- Impairs concentration and memory

## Why Pilates Reduces Stress

### Demands Full Presence

When you're on the reformer, following instructions, synchronising breath with movement, and maintaining correct alignment — there's simply no mental
space left for worry. This is what many instructors call "meditation in motion."

### Lowers Cortisol and Raises Endorphins

All exercise raises endorphins, but Pilates does it uniquely: without the load and pain of intense training. After a Pilates session, cortisol levels
drop and we feel calm yet energised.

### Activates the Parasympathetic Nervous System

Pilates' emphasis on deep, controlled breathing directly activates the body's "rest mode" — the parasympathetic system — helping the body exit chronic
stress.

### Releases Accumulated Physical Tension

Mental stress is stored in the body — in the shoulders, neck, and jaw. Pilates' flexibility and stretching work releases this physical tension, which
directly affects mental state.

## What Science Says

- A 2018 study found that after 8 weeks of Pilates, participants reported a 35% reduction in anxiety levels
- Further research shows significant improvement in **sleep quality** — one of the most powerful tools for managing stress
- Cortisol levels dropped significantly in people who trained Pilates regularly over 3 months
